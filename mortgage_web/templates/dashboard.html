🏠 30-Year Fixed Mortgage Rate
The average interest rate for a 30-year fixed-rate mortgage in the United States. This is the rate most homebuyers use when financing a home purchase.
Why It Matters
This directly determines your monthly payment. A 1% difference on a \$400,000 loan equals ~\$240/month or ~\$86,000 over the life of the loan.
What to Watch For
📉 Falling rates: Good time to lock in a rate or refinance.
📈 Rising rates: Consider locking sooner if buying.

📈 10-Year Treasury Yield
The yield on U.S. 10-year government bonds. Mortgage rates closely follow this benchmark, typically with a 1.5-2% spread above it.
Why It Matters
Lenders use the 10Y Treasury as a baseline. When it rises, mortgage rates usually follow within days. It's a leading indicator of where rates are heading.
What to Watch For
📉 Yield dropping: Mortgage rates likely to decrease soon.
📈 Yield rising: Consider locking your rate now.
📊 Spread widening: Lenders see more risk in the market.

📉 MOVE Index (Bond Volatility)
The Merrill Lynch Option Volatility Estimate measures expected volatility in the U.S. Treasury market. Think of it as the "VIX for bonds."
Why It Matters
High volatility means uncertainty. Lenders add a risk premium when volatility is high, making mortgages more expensive.
What to Watch For
📉 MOVE below 100: Low volatility = better mortgage rates.
📈 MOVE above 120: High uncertainty = wider spreads.
⏳ Falling MOVE: Rates may stabilize soon.
